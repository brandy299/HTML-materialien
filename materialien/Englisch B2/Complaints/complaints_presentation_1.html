Office Design Ltd. · Bristol Warehouse

Input — Types of Complaint

What Customers
Complain About

01
Wrong Goods

Items delivered do not match the order — wrong model, colour, size, or article number.

"We ordered item #A-204 in beige — you sent #A-204 in grey."
02
Damaged Goods

Items arrived broken, scratched, stained, or unusable — often due to poor packaging.

"Ten chairs have visible damage to the upholstery, apparently due to inadequate packing."
03
Late / Incomplete Delivery

Goods arrived later than agreed, or part of the consignment is missing.

"The delivery arrived five days after the agreed date with 15 items missing."
04
Quality Below Standard

Goods do not meet the agreed specification, sample, or catalogue description.

"The fabric quality differs considerably from the approved sample."
Input — How to Write a Complaint

Structure of a
Complaint Email

SectionPurposeExample phrase
Reference Identify the order and delivery "We refer to our order No. OD-447 dated 3 May …"
Acknowledge receipt Confirm delivery arrived "The consignment was received on 10 May."
State the problem Describe clearly what is wrong "We regret to inform you that 10 chairs arrived damaged due to insufficient packaging."
Provide evidence Mention photos, packing list, etc. "Please find enclosed photos of the damaged items."
Suggest adjustment State what you expect "We would ask you to arrange an immediate replacement delivery."
Closing / tone Stay firm but professional "We trust you will deal with this matter promptly."
Golden Rule

Always be firm but polite. A complaint is a business communication — not a personal attack. Aggressive language damages the relationship without solving the problem.

Input — Types of Adjustment (Kulanzregelung)

How Suppliers
Can Adjust

🔄
Replacement

A new delivery of identical goods to replace the faulty or missing items.

🚚
Express Delivery

Fastest possible dispatch of the correct or replacement goods, at the supplier's expense.

💰
Discount

A price reduction allowing the customer to keep the substandard goods at a lower cost.

🔧
Repair

Restoring damaged goods to working condition at the supplier's expense.

🔀
Substitute

A delivery of similar (not identical) products when the original is unavailable.

❌
Release from Contract

Terminating the supplier relationship due to persistent failure — last resort.

Kulanz / Goodwill Gesture

A supplier may also offer a goodwill gesture — e.g. a voucher, free gift, or partial refund — even if not legally required, to preserve the business relationship.

Language Focus — Complaint & Adjustment Phrases

Useful Language

Making a complaint (customer)
"We regret to inform you that the consignment was not in order."
"We refer to our order No. … and wish to draw your attention to …"
"We must insist on an immediate replacement / full refund."
Suggesting an adjustment (customer)
"We would be prepared to keep the goods if you grant us a discount of …%."
"Please arrange for the missing items to be delivered at your expense."
Responding to a complaint (supplier)
"Please accept our sincere apologies for the inconvenience caused."
"We have investigated the matter and found that …"
"The correct items have already been arranged for immediate dispatch."
Goodwill & closing
"As a gesture of goodwill, we are pleased to offer you a 10% discount."
"We assure you that this will not happen again."
Input — Complaining & Adjusting by Phone

On the Phone:
Firm but Polite

Caller — Making the complaint
"I'm calling about our order No. … — something went wrong."
"When we unpacked the goods, we found that two cases were missing."
"We need them urgently — could you send them out today?"
"A 5% discount isn't enough — we'd need at least 15%."
Supplier — Handling the complaint
"I'm sorry to hear that — let me check your order right away."
"I really must apologise for that mistake."
"I'll send out the missing items as soon as possible."
"Let me check with my manager and get back to you today."
Common Mistakes to Avoid

✗ "I refer to our order about" → ✓ for  ·  ✗ "replace by" → ✓ with  ·  ✗ "at our risk and expense" ✓ correct  ·  ✗ "the damages are" → ✓ the damage is
