Bridging Small Talk → Business

"That actually brings me to …"

Uses the client's remark as a springboard into your topic

"Speaking of which, …"

Smooth link between two related topics

"Which is exactly why I wanted to …"

Strong bridge — echoes the client's own words

Making & Following Up Enquiries

"I'm calling to enquire about …"

Standard opening for a telephone enquiry

"I'm following up on my previous email …"

Returning to an earlier contact

"Could you elaborate on …?"

Asking for more detail — formal

"I'll get back to you by [day]."

Committing to a response — always give a clear deadline

Emma & Mr Weber — Office Design Ltd.

Emma: Good morning, Office Design Ltd., Emma Hansen speaking. How may I help you?
Mr Weber: Good morning. This is Klaus Weber from Weber & Partner Consulting. I'm calling to enquire about your new ergonomic office chair range.
Emma: Thank you for getting in touch. How was your morning? I heard the traffic was rather bad today.
Mr Weber: Yes, a complete nightmare! But I've been working from home a lot more recently — which is exactly why I'm looking into ergonomic solutions.
Emma: "That actually brings me to what we can offer you." We've just launched our ComfortPro range for home offices. What exactly are you looking for?
Mr Weber: We need to furnish 30 home office workstations. I'd like to know the specifications, availability, and receive a quotation for 30 units.
Emma: Of course. Are there specific features you require?
Mr Weber: Adjustable lumbar support and armrests as a minimum.
Emma: Noted. I'll prepare a quotation and product specifications. May I get back to you by Friday?
Mr Weber: Perfect. My email is k.weber@weberpartner.de.
