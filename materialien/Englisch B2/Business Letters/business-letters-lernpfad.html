📚 Input – Part 2

The Language of Business Letters

Standard phrases make your letters sound professional. Study the tabs below.

Opening Lines

We are writing to enquire about … → Standard opening for an enquiry
With reference to your letter of 10 March … → Referring to previous correspondence
Thank you for your enquiry of 12 March regarding … → Acknowledging a received letter
Further to our telephone conversation today, … → Following up on a phone call

Referring to Attachments & Orders

Please find enclosed our current catalogue …→ Mentioning an enclosure
As requested, we enclose …→ Responding to a request for documents
We are pleased to confirm your order no. 2901-D for …→ Order confirmation
With regard to order no. … dated …, we wish to inform you that …→ Referring to a specific order

Making Polite Requests

We would be grateful if you could …→ Polite request (preferred at B2)
Could you please send us your current price list?→ Direct but polite request
Please do not hesitate to contact us should you have any queries.→ Standard offer of further help
We would appreciate it if you could reply by 30 March.→ Requesting a deadline

Closing Sentences

We look forward to hearing from you.→ Most common closing sentence
We look forward to receiving your order.→ For offers / catalogues
We trust that this information is of use to you.→ Formal, slightly old-fashioned but correct
Should you require any further information, please feel free to contact us.→ Offering further assistance

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

✗
Dear Sir, dear Madam → Dear Sir or Madam
✗
Dear Mister Jones → Dear Mr Jones
✗
Yours sincerely (after Dear Sir or Madam) → Yours faithfully
✗
we received your letter → Thank you for your letter of … (always start with capital)
✗
Please notice that … → Please note that …
✗
I look forward to hear from you → I look forward to hearing from you (gerund!)

📋 Task (AFB II/III)

You work in the Export Department at Office Design Ltd., Bayrische Str. 400, 44339 Dortmund, Germany (email: export@officedesign.ger). Your manager is Ms Petra Kahl.

On 10 March 2026, you received an enquiry from Mr David Clarke, Purchasing Manager at Greenfield Supplies Ltd., 12 Rosebery Avenue, Edinburgh, EH3 6AT, United Kingdom. He is interested in your ergonomic chair collection (catalogue no. EF-200).

Write a formal reply (today's date) in which you: (1) thank Mr Clarke for his enquiry, (2) confirm the items are available, (3) enclose your current catalogue and price list, (4) state your standard payment terms are 30 days net, (5) offer further assistance and close politely.

📄 Model Answer

Office Design Ltd.
Bayrische Str. 400 · 44339 Dortmund · Germany
Tel: +49 231 809871 · export@officedesign.ger

Ref: PK/TM

17 March 2026

Mr David Clarke
Purchasing Manager
Greenfield Supplies Ltd.
12 Rosebery Avenue
EDINBURGH
EH3 6AT
VEREINIGTES KÖNIGREICH

Re: Your enquiry of 10 March 2026 regarding ergonomic chairs (cat. no. EF-200)

Dear Mr Clarke,

Thank you for your enquiry of 10 March 2026 regarding our ergonomic chair collection (catalogue no. EF-200). We are pleased to confirm that all items are currently in stock and available for immediate delivery.

As requested, we enclose our current catalogue and price list for your information. Our standard payment terms are 30 days net from the date of invoice.

Please do not hesitate to contact us should you require any further information. We look forward to receiving your order.

Yours sincerely,

[Signature]
Petra Kahl (Ms)
Export Manager
Office Design Ltd.

Encs: Catalogue (EF-200), Price List
