Section III

Salutation & Complimentary Close

These two elements are always linked — choose one pair and stick to it.

Key Rule: "In most cases the salutation and complimentary close can be written without punctuation. They are linked — whichever salutation you use determines which close to use."
Dear Sir or Madam
→
Yours faithfully
Formal · name unknown
Dear Mr / Ms Green
→
Yours sincerely
Formal · most common
Dear Ms Green
→
Best wishes
Semi-formal
Dear Bill / Dear Sarah
→
Best regards / Regards
Informal
— ✦ —

Choosing the right title

Ms

Preferred in business. Marital status unknown or not relevant. Safe in all formal contexts.

Mrs / Miss

Only use if the woman herself uses that title. Mrs = married · Miss = unmarried. Never guess.

Mr

For men. Marital status does not affect the title in English business correspondence.

⚠ No punctuation

Write Dear Ms Carter — not Dear Ms Carter,. Don't forget the s in Yours.

The Inside Address

Always include the country when writing abroad. UK addresses follow specific conventions.

✗ Common mistakes

Green Furniture Solutions
47 Bridge Street
manchester ← not in CAPS
M2 3AB ← not on own line
VEREINIGTES KÖNIGREICH ← German!

✓ Correct version

Green Furniture Solutions
47 Bridge Street
MANCHESTER
M2 3AB
UNITED KINGDOM

UK Address Rules — Remember

✦ Town/city in CAPITAL LETTERS
✦ Postcode on its own line
✦ House number before street name
✦ Country in the language of the letter
