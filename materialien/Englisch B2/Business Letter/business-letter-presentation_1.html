📥 Input · Parts 1–3

Letterhead, Reference & Date

1
Letterhead
  • Company name & logo
  • Full postal address (incl. country)
  • Tel / Fax / Email / Website
  • Often printed / pre-designed
Office Design Ltd.
15 Harbour Walk, LONDON
EC3R 7AB · UNITED KINGDOM
info@officedesign.co.uk
2
Reference (Ref.)

Shows who wrote and who typed the letter (initials). Used when replying.

Our ref: SM/TL
Your ref: GF/KP

SM = Sarah Mitchell (author) · TL = Tina Lorenz (typist)

⚠ Cornelsen note: "TL signed the letter and BK typed it." (p. 90)
3
Date

Always write the month as a word to avoid confusion between British and American English.

08/09/25 8 September 2025

08/09 = September 8 (US) vs. 8 October (UK) — ambiguous!

Rule: day (figures) – month (word) – year (four figures)
Cornelsen – Focus on Business (p. 90–91): "An all-figure date can be misunderstood. The month should always be written out and the year should be a four-figure number."
📥 Input · Parts 4–6

Special Marking, Inside Address & Attention Line

4
Special Marking AFB I

Placed above the inside address. Visible in a window envelope.

URGENT CONFIDENTIAL PRIVATE AND CONFIDENTIAL AIR MAIL RECORDED DELIVERY
5
Inside Address

The recipient's full address. Always include the country when writing abroad.

Green Furniture Solutions
47 Bridge Street
MANCHESTER
M2 3AB
UNITED KINGDOM

⚠ UK addresses: town/city in CAPITALS, postcode on its own line. House number before street name.

6
Attention Line (Attn.)

Placed below the inside address. Directs the letter to a specific person within the company.

Attn. Ms McDermott, Marketing Department
If Attn. is used: the letter may be opened by the post department and forwarded.
If the name is in the address: only that person (or their secretary) may open it.

Window Envelope Logic

window
CONFIDENTIAL
Green Furniture Solutions
47 Bridge Street · M2 3AB
Attn. Ms McDermott
📥 Input · Parts 7 & 10

Salutation & Complimentary Close

"The salutation and complimentary close are linked." — Cornelsen, p. 92

Salutation Complimentary Close Register When to use
Dear Sir or Madam Yours faithfully Formal Name of person is unknown
Dear Mr / Ms Green Yours sincerely Formal Most common form in business
Dear Mr / Ms Green Best wishes Semi-formal Know the person well
Dear Bill / Dear Sarah Best regards / Regards Informal Close business friends

Choosing the Right Title (Ms / Mrs / Miss / Mr)

Ms (preferred in business)

Marital status unknown · Always appropriate · Cornelsen: "preferred form of address in business correspondence"

Mrs / Miss

Only if the woman herself uses that title. Mrs = married · Miss = unmarried

Mr

For men regardless of marital status.

⚠ No punctuation

"In most cases the salutation and complimentary close can be written without punctuation." (Cornelsen, p. 92)

📥 Input · Parts 8–12

Subject Line, Body, Signature & Enclosures

8
Subject Line
  • • Written in bold or underlined
  • • Starts with a capital letter
  • • Rest in lower case (except proper nouns)
  • • After the salutation, before the body
Purchase order no. 1249
Confirmation of your enquiry of 12 May 2025
9
Body of the Letter
  • • First word always starts with a capital letter
  • • Paragraphs separated by a blank line
  • • Typically 2–4 paragraphs (opening – main – closing)
  • • All lines start at the left margin (block style)
11
Signature

"Signatures are often hard to read, so write the signatory's name and position under the signature." — Cornelsen

Yours sincerely
Office Design Ltd.

Sarah Mitchell

Sarah Mitchell (Ms)
Sales Department

Women may add preferred title in brackets after name.

12
Enclosure(s)

Documents sent with the letter.

Enc

Encs

Enc: Price list

Encs: Catalogue; Terms & Conditions
