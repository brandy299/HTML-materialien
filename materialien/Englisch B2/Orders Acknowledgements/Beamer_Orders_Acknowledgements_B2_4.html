Slide 3 · Definitions
Definitions

What are Orders & Acknowledgements?

📄
Order (Bestellung)

A formal request to purchase specific goods or services after accepting a supplier's offer. Once acknowledged, a binding contract exists.

✅
Acknowledgement of Order (Auftragsbestätigung)

The seller's written confirmation that the order has been received and accepted. Must confirm all key details.

⚠️
Key Legal Point

Any discrepancy between order and acknowledgement must be flagged immediately. Silence = acceptance of the seller's revised terms.

Four Types of Orders

tap
Trial Order
Probeauftrag
Testing a new supplier before committing to larger volumes.
"We would like to place a trial order for 60 chairs to assess quality before committing to a regular supply agreement."
tap
Repeat Order
Nachbestellung
Reordering the same goods from a known supplier on the same terms.
"As the goods met our requirements, we would like to place a repeat order on the same terms as before."
tap
Standing Order
Dauerauftrag
Regular automatic delivery at fixed intervals.
"Please arrange monthly delivery of 20 units on the first working day of each month."
tap
Call-off Order
Abrufauftrag
Total quantity agreed; released in batches as needed.
"We confirm an annual call-off of 500 units. Please dispatch the first batch of 100 units by 15 December."

Structure of an Acknowledgement

1
Acknowledge receipt of order
›
'Thank you for your order no. OD-2024-047 of [date].'
2
Confirm all article details + discount
›
'We confirm: 60 × EP3-BLK @ £189.00, less 10% = £10,206.00.'
3
Delivery terms & expected dispatch
›

Use tentative language unless 100% certain.

'We expect to dispatch DDP Dusseldorf within 10 working days.'
4
Payment terms + enclosures
›
'Payment is due within 30 days. We enclose our pro-forma invoice.'
!
If unable to fulfil the order
›
  • Inform buyer immediately
  • Explain the reason honestly
  • Offer substitute, partial delivery, or revised date
'We regret to inform you that [item] is temporarily out of stock. We would like to suggest [alternative] …'
Slide 9 · Register
B2 Style

Tentative vs. Definite Language

Use tentative language when not 100% certain — overpromising damages trust.

"The goods will be dispatched on 10 December."
→ Only if 100% certain.

"We expect to dispatch within 10 working days."
"We anticipate dispatch by 15 December."

"If you can't deliver, tell us."

"Should you be unable to meet the delivery date, please inform us immediately."

💡
Grammar Pattern

Should + subject + infinitive = formal alternative to if. Common in B2 business writing.
