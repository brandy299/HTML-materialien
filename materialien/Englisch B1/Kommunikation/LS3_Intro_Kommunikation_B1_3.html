🎯 Sprachbewusstsein · Language Awareness

British business culture, politeness conventions, register, formal vs. informal language and cross-cultural awareness.

British conventions: Indirect language ("I was wondering if..." instead of "I want...") · Small talk before business · Understatement ("That's not quite right" = it's completely wrong!) · Punctuality is essential in meetings

German vs. British: German business culture tends to be more direct. British English uses softening language extensively — "I'm afraid..." / "I was just wondering..." / "Perhaps we could...". Being aware of this prevents misunderstandings.

Running a Meeting

Opening a meeting — greet everyone, welcome guests, state the purpose and the time available.
Opening
"Good morning everyone. Thank you all for coming — let's make a start."
Guten Morgen zusammen. Vielen Dank, dass Sie alle gekommen sind — fangen wir an.
👆 Click any phrase to reveal the German meaning.
Welcome
"I'd like to welcome [name] who is joining us from our London office."
Ich möchte [Name] herzlich begrüßen, der/die aus unserem Londoner Büro zu uns gestoßen ist.
Time
"We have about 90 minutes today, so I'd like to keep things moving."
Wir haben heute ca. 90 Minuten, daher möchte ich dafür sorgen, dass wir vorankommen.
Apologies
"Tom Fischer sends his apologies — he's unable to join us today."
Tom Fischer lässt sich entschuldigen — er kann heute leider nicht dabei sein.
The agenda — always give a brief overview of what you'll cover and in what order. Ask if anyone wants to add anything.
Overview
"Today's agenda has three points: the Q2 update, the new product range, and AOB."
Die heutige Agenda hat drei Punkte: das Q2-Update, die neue Produktreihe und Sonstiges.
Sequence
"First we'll look at... then we'll move on to... and finally we'll decide on..."
Zuerst schauen wir uns ... an, dann gehen wir zu ... über, und schließlich entscheiden wir über ...
AOB check
"Does anyone have anything they'd like to add before we begin?"
Hat jemand noch etwas hinzuzufügen, bevor wir beginnen?
Hand over
"I'd like to hand over to Sarah, who will take us through the sales figures."
Ich möchte das Wort an Sarah übergeben, die uns durch die Verkaufszahlen führen wird.
Discussion — encourage contributions, keep things on track, and handle disagreement politely and indirectly.
Invite
"What are your thoughts on this, Klaus?"
Was meinen Sie dazu, Klaus? / Was halten Sie davon?
Clarify
"That's a good point. Could you say a bit more about that?"
Das ist ein guter Punkt. Könnten Sie das etwas genauer ausführen?
Disagree
"I'm afraid I have to disagree with that. In my experience..."
Da muss ich leider widersprechen. Meiner Erfahrung nach ...
Redirect
"That's interesting — let's come back to that under AOB. Could we stay with item 2 for now?"
Das ist interessant — kommen wir unter Sonstiges darauf zurück. Könnten wir jetzt bei Punkt 2 bleiben?
Time-check
"We're running a little short on time — I'd like to wrap up this point in the next five minutes."
Wir haben ein wenig weniger Zeit — ich würde diesen Punkt gerne in den nächsten fünf Minuten abschließen.
Decisions & action points — summarise clearly who does what by when. This is what goes in the minutes.
Agree
"I think we're all agreed on that — shall we move on?"
Ich glaube, wir sind uns da alle einig — sollen wir weitermachen?
Summarise
"So to summarise: we've decided to launch the new range in June."
Also zusammenfassend: Wir haben beschlossen, die neue Reihe im Juni einzuführen.
Action
"Sarah, could you action that before our next meeting on the 20th?"
Sarah, könntest du das bis zu unserem nächsten Meeting am 20. erledigen?
Confirm
"Just to confirm the action points — Tom will prepare the report, and Anna will contact the supplier."
Nur zur Bestätigung der Aktionspunkte: Tom bereitet den Bericht vor und Anna nimmt Kontakt mit dem Lieferanten auf.
Closing — thank participants, confirm the next steps and the date of the next meeting.
Close
"I think that covers everything on today's agenda."
Ich glaube, damit haben wir alles auf der heutigen Tagesordnung abgedeckt.
Next mtg
"Our next meeting will be on Tuesday the 20th at 10 am. I'll send a calendar invite."
Unser nächstes Meeting findet am Dienstag, dem 20., um 10 Uhr statt. Ich sende eine Kalendereinladung.
Minutes
"The minutes will be circulated by end of day. Thank you all — that was very productive."
Das Protokoll wird bis Ende des Tages versendet. Vielen Dank an alle — das war sehr produktiv.

🇬🇧 British Small Talk — Why It Matters

Before any British business meeting, expect 3–5 minutes of small talk. This is not wasted time — it builds trust and sets a positive tone. Skipping it feels abrupt and unfriendly to a British colleague.

✅
Safe topics
  • The journey ("Did you have a good trip?")
  • The weather ("Typical British weather!")
  • The weekend / local events
  • Food & restaurants
  • The city / area
❌
Avoid
  • Age, salary, religion, politics
  • "How much do you earn?"
  • Personal problems / health
  • Jumping straight to business
  • Controversial opinions
Start
"Did you have a good journey down from London?"
Hatten Sie eine gute Reise von London hierher?
Respond
"Yes, it was fine thanks — the ICE from Frankfurt only takes about two hours."
Ja, es war gut, danke — der ICE von Frankfurt dauert nur ca. zwei Stunden.
Transition
"Right, shall we make a start?" / "Shall we get down to business?"
Gut, sollen wir anfangen? / Sollen wir zum Geschäftlichen übergehen?
Use this to politely end small talk and open the meeting.

🔄 What is Mediation (Sprachmittlung)?

Mediation means conveying information from one language to another — not translating word for word, but communicating the key meaning in natural, appropriate language for your audience.

1
Read / listen carefully
Identify the essentials: Who? What? When? Why? Is any action needed?
2
Know your audience
Who needs this? What do they need to know? What can you leave out?
3
Convey — don't translate
Use natural English. Adapt the register to the situation (formal/semi-formal).
4
Check completeness
All key points included? Tone appropriate? Action clearly stated?

📌 Example — Mediating a German voicemail

Original (German voicemail)
"Guten Tag, hier ist Frau Müller von Bürospace Berlin. Ich rufe wegen unserer Bestellung vom 3. März an — die Stühle sind noch nicht angekommen und wir brauchen sie dringend bis Freitag."
Your mediation (English, to your manager)
"Ms Müller from Bürospace Berlin called about her March 3rd order — the chairs still haven't arrived and she needs them urgently by Friday. She'd like a callback as soon as possible."

✈️ Travel Planning & Appointments

Business travel requires precise language for bookings, timetables, itineraries and confirmation emails.

Book hotel
"I'd like to book a single room for two nights, from 14th to 16th May."
Ich möchte ein Einzelzimmer für zwei Nächte buchen, vom 14. bis 16. Mai.
Confirm
"I am writing to confirm our meeting on Thursday 18th May at 10 am at our Düsseldorf office."
Ich schreibe, um unser Treffen am Donnerstag, 18. Mai, um 10 Uhr in unserem Düsseldorfer Büro zu bestätigen.
Reschedule
"I'm afraid I need to reschedule our appointment. Would Friday at 2 pm work for you instead?"
Ich muss unseren Termin leider verschieben. Würde Freitag um 14 Uhr für Sie passen?
Timetable
"The train departs at 07:42 and arrives in Amsterdam at 10:18, with one change at Cologne."
Der Zug fährt um 07:42 ab und kommt um 10:18 in Amsterdam an, mit einmaligem Umsteigen in Köln.
Itinerary
"Please find attached the travel itinerary for your trip to Amsterdam next week."
Im Anhang finden Sie die Reiseroute für Ihre Reise nach Amsterdam nächste Woche.

📋 Writing Meeting Minutes (Protokoll)

Minutes are a written record of what was discussed, decided and assigned in a meeting. They must be clear, concise and objective. Here's a professional example:

Meeting Minutes — Office Design Ltd.
Meeting:Weekly Team Meeting, Düsseldorf Office Date:Tuesday, 6 May 2025 · 10:00 am Present:P. Webb (Chair), M. Bauer, S. Richter, [Your Name] (Minutes) Apologies:T. Fischer (illness)
1. Matters arising from last meeting

P. Webb confirmed that the new furniture catalogue had been sent to all key clients on 28 April.

2. Q2 Sales Update

M. Bauer reported that Q2 sales were up 12% compared to Q1, driven mainly by ergonomic chair sales. The team agreed this was a positive result. ACTION M. Bauer to prepare a detailed breakdown report by Friday 16 May.

3. New Product Range — June Launch

P. Webb announced the planned launch of the ActiveDesk standing desk range in June. S. Richter raised a concern about stock availability. ACTION S. Richter to confirm stock levels with the logistics team by 9 May.

4. AOB · Next Meeting

Next meeting: Tuesday 20 May, 10:00 am. Minutes to be circulated by P. Webb by end of day.

⚠️ Key rule: Minutes use past tense and full names. Write "Ms Webb confirmed..." — not "she says...". Use ACTION tags to mark tasks with deadlines.
📥 Grammar Focus

Reported Speech

Reported speech is essential for writing minutes and mediating. You convey what someone said — without quoting them word for word.

Indirekte Rede (Reported Speech) brauchst du für Protokolle und Sprachmittlung. Du gibst wieder, was jemand gesagt hat — ohne wörtlich zu zitieren.

🇩🇪 Auf Deutsch erklärt:
Im Englischen verschiebst du die Zeit um eine Stufe in die Vergangenheit:
„is" → was · „will" → would · „can" → could · „have done" → had done
Außerdem änderst du die Pronomen: I → he/she · we → they · my → his/her

The Two Key Rules

Rule 1 — Tense shift
Present → Past
Will → Would
Can → Could
Have → Had
Rule 2 — Pronoun shift
I → he / she / they
We → they
My → his / her / their
Our → their
Direct → Reported Speech · Examples
Direct: "The order is ready."
She said (that) the order was ready.
Direct: "We will send the catalogue tomorrow."
M. Bauer said they would send the catalogue the following day.
Direct: "I can attend the conference."
She confirmed that she could attend the conference.
Direct: "Can you prepare the report?"
P. Webb asked whether S. Richter could prepare the report.
Direct: "We have decided to postpone the launch."
The team announced that they had decided to postpone the launch.

Useful reporting verbs for minutes

stated / reported / confirmed — neutral facts
announced / presented — new information
agreed / decided / resolved — decisions
suggested / proposed / recommended — ideas
raised / pointed out / noted — concerns
asked whether / enquired — questions
📌 In practice: When you write "Ms Webb confirmed that the report would be ready by Friday" — you're using reported speech. This is exactly how professional meeting minutes are written.
