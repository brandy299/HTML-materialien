What will you learn?

👂 Rezeption · Reception

Understand voicemails, hotel brochures, timetables, catalogues, meeting reports and conference information.

Examples: Listening to a client's voicemail about a delivery · Reading a conference programme · Understanding a train timetable from a booking site · Processing a hotel's cancellation policy

Key B1 skill: You don't need to understand every word — focus on the key information.

✍️ Produktion · Production

Write voicemail messages, short notes, travel plans, appointment schedules and meeting minutes.

Examples: Leaving a voicemail for a colleague · Writing a short email confirming a hotel booking · Creating a travel itinerary · Writing meeting minutes (Protokoll) from notes

Key B1 skill: Use clear, structured language. Templates help — you'll build your own.

🗣️ Interaktion · Interaction

Lead structured conversations: telephone calls, small talk, and contributions in meetings.

Examples: Chairing a team meeting · Making small talk with a British visitor before the meeting starts · Taking part in a conference call · Booking appointments by phone

Key B1 skill: Knowing how to start, develop AND close a conversation professionally.

🔄 Mediation · Sprachmittlung

Convey German telephone calls and meeting reports into English — and vice versa.

Examples: Summarising a German internal meeting report for your English colleague · Explaining a German client's voicemail to your British manager · Conveying the key points of a German conference agenda in English

Key B1 skill: Don't translate word for word — convey the meaning, tone and purpose.

🎯 Sprachbewusstsein · Language Awareness

Learn British business culture: politeness conventions, register, formal vs. informal language, and cross-cultural communication styles.

British conventions: Indirect language ("I was wondering if..." instead of "I want...") · Small talk before business · Understatement ("That's not quite right" = it's completely wrong!) · Punctuality and formality in meetings

German vs. British: German business culture tends to be more direct. British English uses softening language extensively. Being aware of this helps you avoid misunderstandings.

📥 Learn · Small Talk & Mediation

🇬🇧 British Small Talk — The Unwritten Rules

Before any British business meeting, there will be 3–5 minutes of small talk. This is NOT wasted time — it builds trust and sets a positive tone. Skipping it feels rude!

✅ SAFE TOPICS
  • The journey / travel ("Did you have a good trip?")
  • The weather ("Typical British weather!")
  • The weekend ("Did you do anything nice?")
  • Local events / the area ("Have you been to Düsseldorf before?")
  • Food / restaurants ("I'd recommend the...")
❌ AVOID
  • Age, salary, religion, politics
  • "How much do you earn?"
  • Personal problems / health
  • Controversial opinions on Brexit
  • Jumping straight to business
Transition
"Shall we make a start?" / "Right, shall we get down to business?"
Sollen wir anfangen? / Sollen wir zum Geschäftlichen übergehen?
Use this to politely end small talk and start the meeting.
Respond
"Yes, the journey was fine, thanks — the train from Frankfurt takes only about 2 hours."
Ja, die Reise war gut, danke — der Zug von Frankfurt dauert nur ca. 2 Stunden.

🔄 What is Mediation (Sprachmittlung)?

Mediation means conveying information from one language to another — not translating word by word, but communicating the key meaning appropriately for your audience.

1
Read / Listen carefully
Identify the key information: Who? What? When? Why? Any action needed?
2
Identify your audience
Who needs this information? What do they need to know? What can you leave out?
3
Convey — don't translate
Use natural English. Adapt the register (formal/informal) to match the situation.
4
Check for completeness
Have you included all the key points? Is the tone right? Is it clear?

📌 Example — Mediating a voicemail

Original (German voicemail)
"Guten Tag, hier ist Frau Müller von der Firma Bürospace Berlin. Ich rufe wegen unserer Bestellung vom 3. März an — die Stühle sind noch nicht angekommen und wir brauchen sie dringend bis Freitag."
Your mediation (English, to your manager)
"Ms Müller from Bürospace Berlin called. She's chasing her order from 3rd March — the chairs haven't arrived yet and she needs them by Friday. She'd like us to call back urgently."

✈️ Travel & Appointment Language

Business travel requires precise language for bookings, timetables, itineraries and confirmations.

Booking
"I'd like to book a single room for two nights from 14–16 May."
Ich möchte ein Einzelzimmer für zwei Nächte vom 14.–16. Mai buchen.
Confirm
"I am writing to confirm our meeting on Thursday 18 May at 10 am."
Ich schreibe, um unser Treffen am Donnerstag, 18. Mai um 10 Uhr zu bestätigen.
Reschedule
"I'm afraid I need to reschedule our appointment. Would Thursday at 2pm work instead?"
Leider muss ich unseren Termin verschieben. Würde Donnerstag um 14 Uhr passen?
Itinerary
"Please find attached the travel itinerary for your trip to Amsterdam."
Im Anhang finden Sie die Reiseroute für Ihre Reise nach Amsterdam.
Timetable
"The train departs at 07:42 and arrives in Amsterdam at 10:18, with one change in Cologne."
Der Zug fährt um 07:42 ab und kommt um 10:18 in Amsterdam an, mit einmal Umsteigen in Köln.

📋 Writing Meeting Minutes (Protokoll)

Minutes are a written record of what was discussed and decided in a meeting. They must be clear, concise and objective.

📄 Structure of Meeting Minutes
Meeting:Weekly Team Meeting — Office Design Ltd., Düsseldorf Date:Tuesday, 6 May 2025, 10:00 am Present:P. Webb (Chair), M. Bauer, S. Richter, [Your Name] (Minutes) Apologies:T. Fischer
1. MATTERS ARISING FROM LAST MEETING
P. Webb confirmed that the new furniture catalogue has been sent to all clients.
2. Q2 SALES UPDATE
M. Bauer presented the Q2 figures. Sales are up 12% compared to Q1. ACTION:An "action" in minutes = a task assigned to someone with a deadline. M. Bauer to prepare detailed report by 15 May.
3. AOB (Any Other Business)
Next meeting: Tuesday 20 May, 10:00 am. Minutes to be circulated by P. Webb.

⚠️ Key rule: Minutes use reported speech and past tense. Write "P. Webb confirmed..." not "P. Webb confirms..." — and always "Ms Webb" not "she".
