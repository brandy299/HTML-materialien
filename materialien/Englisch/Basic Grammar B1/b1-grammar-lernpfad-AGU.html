Modal Verbs — Key Rules

📐 Form:

Subject + modal + infinitive (no "to")

⚠️ No "-s" in 3rd person: She must send the invoice. (NOT: she musts)

ModalMeaningBusiness Example
must strong obligation / necessity "You must confirm the order."
should advice / recommendation "We should reply within 24 hours."
might possibility (less certain) "The shipment might be delayed."
can ability / permission "I can arrange the meeting for Monday."

🔄 Negative Forms:

  • • mustn't = it is forbidden: "You mustn't share the client data."
  • • don't have to = it is not necessary: "You don't have to work on Saturday."
  • • shouldn't = advice against: "We shouldn't accept late payments."

Passive Voice — Key Rules

📐 Form:

Subject + be (conjugated) + past participle

📝 by + agent is optional: "The contract was signed by the manager."

TenseActivePassive
Present Simple We send invoices weekly. Invoices are sent weekly.
Past Simple They delivered the chairs. The chairs were delivered.
Pres. Perfect Someone has confirmed the order. The order has been confirmed.
Modal + Passive We must check all invoices. All invoices must be checked.

💼 Why use the passive in business?

  • ✅ When the action is more important than the person doing it
  • ✅ To sound formal and impersonal in emails and reports
  • ✅ When you don't know who did the action

Compare: "I made a mistake." vs. "A mistake was made." (more professional!)

Present Perfect vs. Simple Past

Present Perfect

have/has + past participle

  • 🔗 Connection to now
  • 📅 No specific time given
  • 🆕 Recent news / results

"We have received your order." (just now / result matters)

Simple Past

verb + -ed / irregular

  • 📍 Specific time in past
  • ⏰ With: yesterday, last week, in 2022, at 3 pm

"We received your order on Monday." (specific time)

🔑 Time Signal Words

Present Perfect:

just already yet ever never recently so far

Simple Past:

yesterday last week in 2022 at 9 am ago

💡 Quick contrast (Business):

"I have just sent the proposal." → focus on NOW (it's done!)

"I sent the proposal yesterday at 4 pm." → specific past time
